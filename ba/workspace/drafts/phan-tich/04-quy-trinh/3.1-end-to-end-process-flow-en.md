#### 3.1.2. Dispatch release

##### Authorized dispatch release operators:

Only flight dispatchers with valid and qualified Dispatch Release licenses (FD certification) are permitted to perform dispatch releases and only their names shall be listed on the OFP.

##### Responsibilities:

DSP performing Dispatch Release (electronic or paper-based) shall perform the following:

> \+ Produce the OFP and be fully responsible for their output;
>
> \+ Be responsible for the ATC files for assigned flights;
>
> \+ Prepare, update, upload, and verify flight documentation in VNA-approved systems and ensure that all flight documents under the dispatcher’s responsibility are accurate, complete and up to date before performing the Dispatch Release.
>
> \+ Dispatch Release must not be performed later than: for domestic flights: ETD-60m; for international flights: ETD-90m.

Absolutely do not perform Dispatch Release until all factors related to flight safety, mission completion capability, etc., are ensured, or until any issues remain unclear or thoroughly resolved.

##### Dispatch Release between shifts:

Previous shift DSP:

> \+ Upload documents to EFB as usual.
>
> \+ Dispatch Releases are only permitted for flights scheduled to depart after the end of the 90m shift.
>
> \+ Hand over and highlight flights uploaded to EFB but not yet dispatched.

Next-shift DSP:

> \+ Receive shift handover and highlight flights uploaded to EFB but not yet dispatched.
>
> \+ Responsible for updating OFPs, flight documents, crew briefings, etc., for flights that have not yet departed since taking over the shift.
>
> \+ OFPs created since taking over the shift (new OFPs or updated OFPs) must include the name of the FD who performed the refresh/update.
>
> \+ Responsible for performing dispatch releases on all OFPs uploaded to EFB during the shift (new or updated OFPs).

#### 3.1.4. Flight watch/ En-route monitoring

##### Purpose:

To ensure that all weather and/or NOTAM changes that may impact on the continued and planned operations are monitored and action taken if necessary;

To ensure all ACARS received from the crew are monitored and action taken if necessary;

To ensure all SITA messages received are monitored and action taken if necessary.

##### Flight watch and en-route monitoring procedure

The primary system used for flight watch and en-route monitoring is VNA-approved system and Flightradar24;

Flight dispatchers shall monitor the progress of flights under his/her responsibility using VNA-approved systems. Flight monitoring includes tracking the progress of the flight from “Off-block” to “On-block”;

The specific position shall monitor NOTAM/ WX data, and incoming ACARS messages from crews; this position shall monitor incoming SITA messages and action those that have any impact on any VNA flights. The staff of this specific position shall evaluate the information received, identify the flights affected, design an action plan and then brief the respective other positions to take any action;

Any ACARS or SATCOM communications should be performed by the specific position;

The flight monitoring and flight watch shall cease once the flight has terminated;

The staff of specific position should brief the DM and DGM OCC regarding any issue that may impact on the operation.

Notes:

> \+ If weather conditions at EDTO ERA deteriorate below landing minima, or the EDTO ERA becomes unavailable for another reason, the staff of specific position shall attempt to select another suitable ERA to support the EDTO route segment and if necessary transmit a revised OFP to the aircraft via ACARS.
>
> \+ If communication between the aircraft and flight dispatcher is lost due to technical limits of communication system, the flight dispatcher shall take any necessary actions to make the data and information which need to be sent to the aircraft available at the next flying area where the communication will be restored. The most reliable method should be used by flight dispatcher is via ATC center of FIRs where the aircraft is flying over. If the communication is lost due to a permanent failure of communication system, the flight dispatcher shall notify the OCC DM and DGM and keep close contact with ATC center over which aircraft will fly, and standby for further actions as demanded by the management of OCC
