### 3.3. VIP flight procedure

#### 3.3.1. Applicability:

The flight dispatchers, who are responsible for the VIP flight, will be approved annually by the CAAV;

The flight dispatchers are assigned specific duties.

#### 3.3.2. Flight route assignment

##### Flight dispatchers – specific role in supervising and monitoring:

> This position takes responsibility for these flights:
>
> \+ Long-haul flights with a flight time of 8 hours or more;
>
> \+ Flights assessed as operationally complex, including operations to airports with limited communication capabilities;
>
> \+ Flights without an accompanying Dispatcher (DSP);
>
> \+ Flights specifically assigned to the Supervisor.

##### Other positions:

> Other positions can take responsibility for these flights:
>
> \+ Flights with a flight time of less than 8 hours;
>
> \+ Flights assigned in accordance with the duty roster.

#### 3.3.4. Guidance on preparing VIP briefing slide

Upon approval of the official routing plan, the assigned flight dispatcher shall commence preparation of the VIP briefing slides using the latest available information at the time of drafting.

The assigned flight dispatcher shall periodically review and update all relevant briefing information and incorporate updates into the slides.

When the VIP preparation briefing schedule for company level is issued, the assigned flight dispatcher shall finalize the presentation (updated to the time of submission) according to the following milestones:

> \+ 72 hours (or 48 hours) prior to the company briefing date: submit the draft slides to the management of department for review and approval; the assigned flight dispatcher shall present the content and revise the slides in accordance with managerial feedback.
>
> \+ 48 hours (or 24 hours) prior to the company briefing date: finalize the slides (after department-level revisions) and submit them to the board of directors of OCC.

On the VIP preparation briefing date, the assigned flight dispatcher shall accompany OCC leadership to provide briefing support as required.

***NOTE:***

> \+ Use a standardized slide template, including consistent layout, typography (font, font size), and background.
>
> \+ Apply consistent terminology and wording conventions across all slides.
>
> \+ Clearly indicate the date and time of the latest information update.
>
> \+ Highlight key information (e.g., bold/mark critical items) where emphasis is required.
>
> \+ Clearly distinguish local time and UTC.
>
> \+ Exercise extreme caution when recording and presenting departure/arrival times, especially for different time zones or flights crossing midnight/date boundaries.
>
> \+ Carefully verify all countries, territories, and areas along the route, including any overlapping territorial waters or disputed jurisdictions.

#### 3.3.6. Post-Flight Actions and Reporting

The assigned flight dispatcher shall prepare and submit a post-flight report to the board of directors of OCC, and shall also complete the VIP flight report for submission to the Corporate Office (Head Office).

Coordinate with the FOE to update the database accordingly, including:

> \+ Changing the flight status from IN back to IS; and
>
> \+ Removing the STS/HEAD indicator, prior to releasing the aircraft back into regular commercial operations.
