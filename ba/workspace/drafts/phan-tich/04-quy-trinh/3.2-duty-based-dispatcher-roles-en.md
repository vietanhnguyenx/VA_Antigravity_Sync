### 3.2. Duty-based dispatcher roles

#### 3.2.1. Flight dispatcher – Specific role in supervising and monitoring

##### Description of main roles:

This position takes responsibility for managing the overall effectiveness of the Vietnam Airlines Flight Dispatch section under the term of the AOC and in accordance with applicable CAAV and company rules and regulations. This position is the key interface with the direct management of assigned leader and other functions within the OCC. This position is designated as a Flight Dispatcher (FD).

##### Responsibilities:

The unit is manned 24 hours per day and 7 days per week.

1)  Procedural compliance & governance:

> \+ Responsible for following the procedures contained in this document unless circumstances dictate otherwise;
>
> \+ Exercising supervisory judgment when circumstances require deviation from standard procedures, and ensuring such deviations are properly assessed, documented, and escalated.

2)  Operational oversight & situational awareness

> \+ Maintain situation awareness regarding weather, NOTAM, MEL/ CDL data and other operational events and ensure such information is accurate and updated;
>
> \+ Monitor all (including VIP and Charter) flights under his/her area of responsibility.

3)  Escalation, reporting & management notification

> \+ Advise the DM, GM and DGM OCD of any event that will or may impact on the flight operations and provide alternative option(s) if applicable;
>
> \+ Advise the DM, GM and DGM OCD of any significant event and any event that may impact the performance of the Flight Dispatch unit.

4)  Coordination and information dissemination

> \+ Provide Maintenance Operations Control (MOC), Control Load Center (CLC), crew tracking, station managers and external unit like AIS and ATC with pertinent information that may impact on the operation from a dispatcher perspective;

\+ Provide MOC, DM and DGM OCC with weather information for stations that are forecasted to have snow, icing and/or freezing precipitations;

\+ Provide timely information to flight crew whenever necessary that may impact on the safety of flight from a dispatcher perspective;

\+ Manage ad-hoc requests from other members of the OCC teams;

5)  Operational problem management

\+ Responsible for identifying and analyzing any operational problems affecting safety or legality that may affect normal operations and co-ordinate and direct corrective action to ensure schedule integrity, safety, security and customer service, as may be required;

6)  Leadership and shift continuity

\+ Ensure that all applicable members of the OCC are aware and updated with any operational factors that have occurred and may affect the safety progress or operations of a flight, including those flights that have been released;

\+ Receive and preside over the shift handover and disseminate instructions to other positions during the shift; preside over handling any situations that arise during the shift; conduct the shift handover at the end of the shift.

##### Qualifications:

This position requires all staff must be at least 21 years of age. Minimum education requirements is University education. Good verbal and written in English. Undergo the fundamental aviation course by aviation institutes from Vietnam or international institutes or FTC, and other training courses as required by the Training Manual of VNA. Hold a Flight dispatcher license by the CAAV. Minimum of 05 years’ experience in a flight dispatcher environment.

#### 3.2.2. Flight dispatcher – Specific role in mastering NOTAM and WX

##### Description of main roles:

This position is responsible for the monitoring, assessment, and management of NOTAM and weather information relevant to Vietnam Airlines flight operations, in accordance with the terms of the AOC and applicable CAAV and company rules and regulations. The position ensures that NOTAM and weather data affecting flight safety, legality, and operational continuity are accurate, current, and appropriately disseminated within the OCC. This position is designated as a Flight Dispatcher (FD).

##### Responsibilities:

The unit is manned 24 hours per day and 7 days per week.

1)  Procedural compliance and governance

\+ Perform all duties in accordance with the procedures and requirements set out in this manual, unless operational circumstances require otherwise.

\+ Exercise professional judgment when deviations from standard procedures are necessary due to operational conditions, ensuring that such deviations are appropriately assessed, documented, and escalated in accordance with established OCC procedures.

2)  NOTAM and WX monitoring

\+ Monitor NOTAM and WX information relevant to Vietnam Airlines flight operations to maintain continuous situational awareness of conditions affecting safety, legality, and operational continuity.

\+ Maintain oversight of NOTAM and WX developments affecting departure, destination, alternate aerodromes, en-route airspace, and relevant FIRs.

3)  Data verification and accuracy control

\+ Verify that NOTAM and WX data obtained from approved sources are accurate, current, and operationally valid.

\+ Identify outdated, conflicting, or incomplete information and take appropriate action to clarify, correct, or escalate such issues.

4)  Operational assessment and impact evaluation

\+ Assess the operational impact of NOTAM and WX conditions on flight planning, routing, alternates, fuel requirements, and in-flight operations.

\+ Support other positions in identifying and mitigating operational risks arising from adverse WX or NOTAM constraints.

5)  Information dissemination and coordination

\+ Disseminate relevant NOTAM and WX information in a timely manner to other flight dispatchers, and other OCC functions, as required.

\+ Ensure that flight crews receive timely and accurate NOTAM and WX updates that may affect flight safety or operational decision-making.

6)  Escalation and reporting

\+ Escalate significant NOTAM or WX-related issues that may impact flight safety, legality, or operational continuity to the appropriate supervisory or management level.

\+ Promptly report abnormal, rapidly changing, or high-risk conditions in accordance with OCC escalation procedures.

7)  Shift continuity

\+ Ensure that significant NOTAM and WX updates, assessments, and operational advisories are properly documented to support traceability, post-flight review, and operational analysis.

\+ Communicate ongoing NOTAM and WX concerns, unresolved issues, and flights requiring continued monitoring during shift handover to ensure continuity of safe operations.

#### 3.2.3. Flight dispatcher – Specific role in controlling automatically calculated rules

##### Description of main roles:

This position is responsible for the monitoring, control, and management of automated operational rules within VNA-approval system, in accordance with the terms of the AOC and applicable CAAV and company rules and regulations. The position ensures that such rules are correctly configured, updated, and applied for each duty period to reflect current NOTAM, weather information, and operational directives, thereby supporting flight safety, regulatory compliance, and operational continuity. This position is designated as a Flight Dispatcher (FD).

##### Responsibilities:

The unit is manned 24 hours per day and 7 days per week.

1)  Procedural compliance and professional judgment

\+ Perform all duties in accordance with approved procedures governing automated operational rules and system configuration.

\+ Exercise professional judgment when automated rules require adjustment due to operational conditions, ensuring that all changes are assessed, justified, documented, and escalated in accordance with OCC governance procedures.

2)  Monitoring and control of automated operational rules

\+ Monitor automated operational rules within VNA-approved systems to ensure correct functionality and continued operational validity.

\+ Maintain oversight of rule execution to confirm that automated actions are applied as intended for each duty period.

\(iii\) Configuration and update of rules

\+ Configure, activate, deactivate, or adjust automated operational rules as required to reflect: current NOTAM; WX conditions; operational directives and OCC instructions.

\+ Ensure that rule configurations are correctly aligned with the applicable operational context of the duty period.

4)  Validation and consistency assurance

\+ Verify that automated rules are applied consistently across flights with similar operational characteristics.

\+ Ensure that newly created or modified rules do not conflict with, override, or duplicate existing rules or actions.

5)  Support to Automated and Manual Flight Planning

\+ Support automated flight planning processes by ensuring that rule-based logic produces operationally valid outputs.

\+ Coordinate with other positions when automated rule outcomes require review, refinement, or manual intervention.

6)  Anomaly detection and corrective action

\+ Identify errors, misconfigurations, or unintended consequences arising from automated rule application.

\+ Implement corrective actions at the individual flight level or rule-configuration level, as appropriate, to maintain safety and compliance.\\

7)  Reporting and escalation

\+ Escalate significant rule-related issues, system conflicts, or risks to flight safety, legality, or operational continuity to the appropriate supervisory or management level.

\+ Provide timely notification when automated rules may impact flight operations or require operational awareness.

8)  Shift continuity

\+ Ensure that all rule changes, adjustments, and exceptions are properly recorded to support traceability, review, audit, and post-operation analysis.

\+ Communicate active rules, temporary configurations, unresolved issues, and flights requiring continued monitoring during shift handover to ensure continuity of safe operations.

\+ Promptly report system anomalies, automation failures, or operational inconsistencies to the appropriate supervisory or higher-level position.

\+ Escalate issues that may affect flight safety, legality, or operational continuity in accordance with established reporting lines.
