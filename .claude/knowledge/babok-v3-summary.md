# BABOK v3 — Knowledge Areas Summary (AGENTS scope)

The IIBA **BABOK Guide v3** defines **6 Knowledge Areas (KAs)** that structure all business-analysis work. Each KA has a defined purpose, expected inputs, signature techniques, and produced outputs.

## 1. Business Analysis Planning & Monitoring (BAPM)

| Aspect | Content |
|---|---|
| **Purpose** | Plan how BA work will be performed; identify stakeholders, governance, performance approach. |
| **Inputs** | Business need, BA performance objectives. |
| **Key techniques** | Stakeholder analysis, RACI matrix, Estimation, Risk assessment, Reviews. |
| **Outputs** | BA Approach, Stakeholder Engagement Approach, Governance Approach, Information Management Approach, BA Performance Assessment. |

## 2. Elicitation & Collaboration

| Aspect | Content |
|---|---|
| **Purpose** | Draw out information from stakeholders and ensure shared understanding. |
| **Inputs** | Business need, stakeholder list. |
| **Key techniques** | Interviews, Workshops, Document analysis, Observation, Surveys, Brainstorming, Focus groups, Prototyping. |
| **Outputs** | Elicitation results (confirmed/unconfirmed), stakeholder concerns. |

## 3. Requirements Life Cycle Management (RLCM)

| Aspect | Content |
|---|---|
| **Purpose** | Manage requirements from inception through retirement: trace, maintain, prioritize, assess change, approve. |
| **Inputs** | Elicitation results, designs. |
| **Key techniques** | Traceability, Prioritization (MoSCoW/Kano), Impact analysis, Item tracking, Reviews. |
| **Outputs** | Traced/Maintained/Prioritized/Approved requirements; Requirements Change Assessment. |

## 4. Strategy Analysis

| Aspect | Content |
|---|---|
| **Purpose** | Identify enterprise needs of strategic importance and develop a strategy to address them. |
| **Inputs** | Business need (high-level), enterprise context. |
| **Key techniques** | SWOT, PESTLE, Business Capability Analysis, Business Model Canvas, Root Cause Analysis, Decision Analysis. |
| **Outputs** | Current State Description, Future State Description, Risk Assessment, Change Strategy. |

## 5. Requirements Analysis & Design Definition (RADD)

| Aspect | Content |
|---|---|
| **Purpose** | Structure elicited info into requirements and designs that describe a valuable solution. |
| **Inputs** | Elicitation results, change strategy, governance approach. |
| **Key techniques** | Modeling (BPMN, UML, ERD), Use Cases, User Stories, Decision Modeling, Data Modeling, Scope Modeling, Acceptance Criteria definition. |
| **Outputs** | Requirements (verified, validated), Designs, Requirements Architecture, Solution recommendations. |

## 6. Solution Evaluation

| Aspect | Content |
|---|---|
| **Purpose** | Measure value delivered by a solution and recommend improvements. |
| **Inputs** | Solution (implemented), business objectives, requirements. |
| **Key techniques** | KPIs/metrics analysis, Root Cause Analysis, Survey, Focus group, Risk analysis. |
| **Outputs** | Solution performance analysis, Recommended actions (enhance/retire/replace). |

## Underlying competencies (cross-KA)

- Analytical thinking & problem solving
- Behavioral characteristics (ethics, personal accountability, trustworthiness)
- Business knowledge (industry, organization, solution, methodology)
- Communication skills (verbal, non-verbal, written, listening)
- Interaction skills (facilitation, leadership & influencing, teamwork, negotiation)
- Tools & technology

## Perspectives (BABOK v3 introduces)

- Agile
- Business Intelligence
- Information Technology
- Business Architecture
- Business Process Management
